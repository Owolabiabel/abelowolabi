About

A snapshot of my academic path, research motivations, and long-term plans for VLSI and superconducting electronics in Nigeria and beyond.

Research

I work on data-driven superconducting logic and VLSI architectures that improve energy efficiency, scalability, and security for future computing systems.

Current focus

Dynamic Single-Flux Quantum (DSFQ) Logic

My research centers on designing and optimizing clock-free, self-resetting DSFQ logic gates and arithmetic units using the MIT-LL SFQ5ee process.

  • Clock-free XOR and AND gates with data-driven triggering.
  • 4-bit all-dynamic SFQ carry-lookahead adder architectures.
  • Path balancing, wave-pipelining, and bias-margin optimization.
  • Reducing Josephson junction count while preserving throughput.
Broader interests

Secure & Energy-Efficient VLSI

Beyond SFQ logic, I am interested in side-channel attacks in VLSI circuits and secure integrated-circuit design for computer systems.

  • Power-analysis-based side-channel vulnerabilities in ICs.
  • VLSI architectures that reduce leakage and attack surfaces.
  • Integration of SFQ-based subsystems into larger computing flows.
  • Research translation to curricula and labs in Nigerian universities.
